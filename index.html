Our research group presently focuses on state-of-the-art energy & photonics applications based on solution-processed semiconductor thin-films (SPSFs). The SPSFs in the present research frame are mostly made of colloidal quantum dots (CQDs), perovskite, or organic materials. Specifically, due to the customized semiconductor properties and solution processibilities, CQDs are very promising in infrared optoelectronic devices such as solar cells and photodetectors. CQD based SPSF with desired electronic properties are obtained mainly via a refined surface engineering as well as optimized deposition conditions. Besides pursuing remarkable device performances, the spontaneous kinetics under different applied conditions and the mechanisms of structure-performance relations of the SPSF are studied by grazing-incidence X-ray scattering (GIXS) techniques and ultrafast spectroscopies in present group labs. The labs are in direct collaborations with Prof. Leifeng Cao at Shenzhen Technology University (SZTU), Prof. Kai Wang at Southern University of Science and Technology (SUSTech), and Prof. Wanqiang Cao at Hubei University (HUBU).
